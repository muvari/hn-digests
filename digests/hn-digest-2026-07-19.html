5 Hardware

494 points · 229 comments · chipweinberger.com

Chip Weinberger reflects on building Jamcorder, an automated piano-recording device, having sold 2,500 units. Contrary to the "hardware is hard" adage, he found manufacturing and assembly went smoothly — the real difficulty was the roughly 200,000 lines of firmware, app, and tooling code, which took over three years. What the comments said

  • The author actively answered questions AMA-style; he named spending too long on firmware — and not building it cross-platform for local testing — as his biggest early near-fatal mistake.
  • On anti-counterfeiting: he uses an STSAFE secure chip (the same one used in Starlink terminals) plus random per-device product keys, similar to iMessage's approach.
  • On compliance, he described FCC-only certification plus CE self-certification as affordable (~$5k) if you choose pre-certified radios, noting most cheap Amazon electronics skip real testing entirely.
  • An EE commenter pushed back hard on the article's central claim, arguing hardware's real difficulty is long, expensive iteration cycles — and that the take only holds for very simple products like this one.
  • Fulfillment was flagged as still a mild pain point, mainly because the company is at a scale most 3PLs don't yet want to service.
